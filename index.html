Judged on tortilla, meat, salsa, and overall soul.

1
Al Pastor de la Calle
The imaginary trompo on Mason Rd
4.9 / 5.0
Pineapple caramelized to perfection, shaved off a rotating trompo that's been spinning since dawn. The corn tortilla is doubled and the salsa verde has just enough heat to remind you you're alive.
Al Pastor Trompo Fuego
2
Birria Dorada
A fictional truck on Cinco Ranch Blvd
4.8 / 5.0
Slow-braised beef dipped in consomme, crisped on the plancha until the edges shatter. Comes with a cup of the braising liquid for dipping. You will get messy. You will not care.
BirriaCrispyConsomme
3
Suadero con Todo
A dream stand near Katy Mills
4.7 / 5.0
The cut nobody talks about but everyone should. Thin-sliced brisket-adjacent beef, cooked low and slow on the flat top. Topped with raw onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime that ties everything together.

Carnitas Michoacanas
Somewhere on FM 1463 (we wish)
4.6 / 5.0
Pork braised in its own fat until it falls apart with a look. Served on a handmade tortilla with pickled red onion and habanero salsa. The kind of taco that makes you close your eyes when you bite.

Camaron a la Diabla
The seafood spot that doesn't exist yet
4.5 / 5.0
Gulf shrimp sauteed in a chile de arbol sauce that builds heat with every bite. Finished with a squeeze of lime, shredded cabbage, and a drizzle of crema. This one fights back.
